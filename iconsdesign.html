Icons

When designing icon sets for your website or your brand, I’ve discovered some fundamental rules in order to design them. On this page I will guide you through them.

1. Consistent weight

Consistency is nothing new within design. When making icons for a brand the logos have to look similar, and in order to do so, the weight, width and height has to be the same throughout. If there is inconsistency, the brand can lose their professional image.

2. Simplicity - Less is more

When it comes to designing icons, minimalism and simplicity is the key. When we are designing icons, our focus should be context, and communicating the action that the icon represents, with fewer design elements. Icons should be reduced to a minimal form, only expressing the essential characteristics.

3. White space

White space is important in all design, also when designing icons. The use of white space will improve the overall experience. An icon should have a balanced white space on both sides. The use of white space also supports the previous rules of sticking to simplicity, we don’t want too many elements that can result in an untidy and confusing icon. Most icons have a small working canvas, and that should be taken into consideration when we are designing them. Once we minimize an icon, we lose white space, and if we have too many elements, the icon will not be as intuitive.

4. Shapes

You might have noticed that a lot of icon sets are shaped geometrically. And there is a reason for that. When designing icons, we should let the freeform spirit go, and stick with geometric shapes. The reason is that these are easier for the user to understand, and that is our main goal. Use shapes as circles, squares, rectangles etc, and center the elements. This will make your icon look more stable and reliable, and the user can easily navigate through your content without being insecure.

5. Colors and contrast

If we’re creating icons for a brand, the color we chose in our icon set should be the same as the brand. Avoid using clashing colors and consider sticking with one color, or maybe a monochromatic style, to maintain the simplicity and minimalistic style. Contrast is also something to keep in mind, avoid using too dark or too light colors. Too dark colors can make it hard for the user to identify what the icon is.

Writing for UX

Not only is the design of the buttons important, but moreover the microcopy is also crucial, or at least there is a lot of potential there. Buttons need action words, and the action word should describe exactly what’s going to happen when you click on it. For example:

  • Learn more
  • Sign up
  • View my account

A rule of thumb when writing the microcopy for our buttons is passing the WHYLTIWLT test, which means: would you like to/I would like to. “Would you like to learn more?”.

Use the potential

The microcopy in our UI is extremely important and there is a lot of potential lying in our choice of words, and we should take advantage of it. Every button, tips, form, 404 pages should have a text. This can make the website more personal and genuine and make it more fun to navigate.
